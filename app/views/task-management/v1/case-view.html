Get a residential assessment (observing the family in an official facility)
Description

If you propose a residential assessment, disclose case papers to possible providers so they can do a viability assessment. Send details of the proposed providers and any viability assessments to the court.

Tell proposed providers what a residential assessment should cover, and that the viability assessment should include:

  1. The type of work that the provider expects to do.
  2. The benefits of the proposed assessment compared to its impact on the child.
  3. The name and CV of the people who will do the assessments.
  4. Timescales for each part of the assessment including assessing the parents alone and with the child.
  5. A breakdown of the cost of each part of the assessment, giving costs of therapy, training or treatment.
Assigned to All parties
